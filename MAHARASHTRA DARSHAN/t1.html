Hazur Sahib Gurudwara

About Hazur Sahib Gurudwara

Hazur Sahib Gurudwara, also known as Takht Sachkhand Shri Hazur Abchalnagar Sahib, is one of the five Takhts (temporal authorities) of Sikhism. It is located in Nanded, Maharashtra, India. The Gurudwara is a significant pilgrimage site for Sikhs, as it is associated with the final years of Guru Gobind Singh Ji, the tenth Sikh Guru.

Historical Significance

The Gurudwara stands on the site where Guru Gobind Singh Ji, the tenth Guru of Sikhism, passed away in 1708. It is renowned for its beautiful architecture and its role in Sikh history. The complex includes the main shrine, the library, and the museum, which houses artifacts related to Sikh history.

Features

The Hazur Sahib Gurudwara is known for its grand architecture, including its impressive gold-plated dome and marble interiors. The complex also features the famous "Sarai" (rest house) where pilgrims can stay. The Gurudwara provides Langar (free community meal) to visitors, reflecting the Sikh principle of selfless service.
